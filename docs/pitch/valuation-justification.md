## 2. Pre-Revenue Valuation Framework

**[VJ-03] How to value a pre-revenue seed company:**

Method 1 — Comparable transactions (done above)
Method 2 — Scorecard method (Berkus)
Method 3 — Forward revenue multiple with risk discount

### Method 2: Scorecard / Berkus Method

The Berkus method assigns value to risk-reduction milestones:

| Value Driver | Max Value | TheEngOrg Score | Awarded |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Sound idea (reduces product risk) | $500K | Strong: market-validated, OSS traction | $425K |
| Prototype / working product | $500K | Strong: 24 agents, 16 skills, live OSS | $475K |
| Quality management team | $500K | Moderate: solo founder, strong technical | $300K |
| Strategic relationships | $500K | Moderate: Prime Ventures warm, 2 pilots | $250K |
| Product rollout / initial sales | $500K | Moderate: 2 pilots converting, no ARR yet | $200K |

**Berkus total: $1.65M** — This is not the valuation. Berkus was designed for $500K checks in 2001. Adjust for market inflation and category premium: multiply by 5x for AI infrastructure.

**Adjusted Berkus: $8.25M** — This independently supports $9M pre-money.

### Method 3: Forward Revenue Multiple with Risk Discount

**[VJ-04]** Expected ARR at Month 24 (base case): $3.74M

Comparable Series A multiples for AI SaaS dev tools at $3M ARR: 10-15x revenue.

Forward valuation: $3.74M x 12x = $44.9M at Month 24.

Risk discount to present value (24 months, execution risk, discount rate 40% for venture-stage risk):

PV = $44.9M / (1.40)^2 = $44.9M / 1.96 = $22.9M

**Risk-discounted forward valuation: $22.9M**

Taking the entry at $9M means you're buying at 39 cents on the forward dollar. That is the investor's margin of safety — and the justification for $9M rather than $20M.

## 3. The Zero-Infrastructure-Cost Premium

**[VJ-05] Why this deserves a higher multiple than traditional SaaS:**

Most software companies at seed are valued on team + idea. Infrastructure-cost structure is not a differentiator because it is not yet visible. At seed stage, all software companies look similar.

TheEngOrg's zero-infrastructure model is immediately visible and immediately defensible:

**Comparable gross margin at seed stage:**

| Company Type | Gross Margin | Infrastructure Position |
|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| Traditional SaaS (Salesforce-style) | 70-75% | Hosts infrastructure |
| AI SaaS with inference (Cursor, GitHub Copilot) | 40-60% | Hosts model inference |
| OSS-led infra (HashiCorp, Grafana Labs) | 75-85% | Some hosting required |
| **TheEngOrg** | **91-94%** | **Zero: customer pays Anthropic** |

**[VJ-06] The margin premium translates to valuation premium:**

Public SaaS companies trade at revenue multiples. The multiple is significantly correlated with gross margin:
- 70% gross margin company: 8-12x revenue multiple at IPO
- 80% gross margin company: 12-18x revenue multiple
- 90%+ gross margin company: 18-25x revenue multiple (rare; mostly pure software)

TheEngOrg's 91-94% gross margin, if sustained through Series A and beyond, justifies a revenue multiple at the top of the SaaS range. At seed, this future multiple compresses to present value. But it argues for valuing the seed entry at the premium end of comparable seed deals — not the discount end.

**[VJ-07] The quantified premium:**

If we use the comparable range of $12-25M pre-money for AI dev tools seed:
- Floor ($12M pre): appropriate for a company with infrastructure cost and typical SaaS margins
- Ceiling ($25M pre): appropriate for a company with differentiated technology, strong team, or traction

TheEngOrg's premium factors:
+ Zero infrastructure cost (structural, not temporary): +$2-4M from floor
+ Sage benchmark performance (1.000): +$1-2M (demonstrated technical depth)
+ OSS distribution in place: +$1-2M (acquisition channel de-risked)
- Solo founder (team risk): -$2-3M from ceiling
- Pre-revenue (no ARR yet): -$2-3M from ceiling

**Net: $9M pre-money is conservatively positioned within the justified range of $10-14M.** This creates investor margin of safety at entry.

## 5. The Category Timing Argument

**[VJ-11] Why now:**

The "AI dev team" category does not yet have a clear winner. GitHub Copilot owns autocomplete. Cursor owns the AI editor. Devin owns the "fully autonomous agent" narrative. No one owns the "AI team simulation with coordination protocols" space.

This is the whitespace TheEngOrg is entering. The window for category definition is approximately 18-24 months (2026-2027). After that, one of three things happens:
1. TheEngOrg has defined the category and owns it (best case)
2. A well-funded competitor has copied the model (competitive risk)
3. Foundation model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI) have built native team simulation (platform risk)

**[VJ-12] The $9M pre-money buys the option on scenario 1 while scenarios 2 and 3 are still open questions.** That option is worth more than $9M if scenario 1 plays out.

## 6. Valuation Conclusion

**[VJ-13] Summary of justification methods:**

| Method | Result | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Comparable transactions | $12-25M range | TheEngOrg at $9M is below floor |
| Berkus (inflation-adjusted) | $8.25M | Supports $9M |
| Forward revenue multiple (discounted) | $22.9M | $9M = 39 cents on the dollar |
| Gross margin premium | Top of peer range | Argues for $10-14M over $9M |

**[VJ-14] $9M pre-money is the right number because:**

1. It is below the comparable transaction range — investors get a margin of safety at entry.
2. It is supported by three independent valuation methods.
3. It creates the Series A return math: 3-8x on paper at Series A from a $9M entry (vs. 1.5-4x from a $18M entry).
4. It is negotiable to $7M floor (stated in business plan) without destroying the company or insulting the category.
5. It accurately prices the current stage: one technical founder, no ARR, 2 pilots starting.

**The risk of asking for $18M pre-money is that it's wrong for the stage and creates Series A return math that doesn't work for seed funds.** The risk of asking for $9M is that you leave value on the table if the round is oversubscribed — a good problem to have.

**Recommendation: Hold $9M pre-money. Accept $7M floor if Prime Ventures requires it. Do not accept below $7M — that creates a dilution structure that impairs the founder's Series A incentives.**
